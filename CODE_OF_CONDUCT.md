# Contributor Covenant Code of Conduct

## Our Pledge

We as members, contributors, and maintainers of **SoundStream** pledge to make participation in this project a harassment-free experience for everyone, regardless of age, body size, visible or invisible disability, ethnicity, sex characteristics, gender identity and expression, level of experience, education, socio-economic status, nationality, personal appearance, race, religion, or sexual identity and orientation.

We are committed to fostering an open, welcoming, diverse, inclusive, and respectful community.

---

## Our Standards

Examples of behavior that contributes to a positive environment include:

- Demonstrating empathy and kindness toward others
- Being respectful of differing opinions, viewpoints, and experiences
- Giving and gracefully accepting constructive feedback
- Taking responsibility for mistakes and learning from them
- Acting in a professional and collaborative manner

Examples of unacceptable behavior include:

- Sexualized language or imagery, or unwelcome sexual attention
- Trolling, insulting or derogatory comments, or personal attacks
- Public or private harassment
- Publishing othersâ€™ private information without explicit permission
- Any behavior that would be considered unprofessional in a workplace

## Enforcement Responsibilities

Project maintainers are responsible for clarifying and enforcing the standards of acceptable behavior. They may take appropriate and fair corrective action in response to any behavior deemed inappropriate, threatening, offensive, or harmful.

Maintainers have the right to remove, edit, or reject comments, commits, issues, pull requests, or other contributions that are not aligned with this Code of Conduct.

---

## Scope

This Code of Conduct applies to all project spaces, including but not limited to:

- GitHub repositories
- Issues and pull requests
- Discussions and code reviews
- Any public communication representing the project

## Enforcement Guidelines

Maintainers will follow these guidelines when determining consequences:

### 1. Correction
**Impact:** Minor inappropriate or unprofessional behavior.  
**Consequence:** A private warning explaining the issue and expected behavior.

### 2. Warning
**Impact:** Repeated or more serious violations.  
**Consequence:** A formal warning and temporary restrictions on interaction.

### 3. Temporary Ban
**Impact:** Serious or sustained violations of community standards.  
**Consequence:** Temporary ban from participation in the project.

### 4. Permanent Ban
**Impact:** Persistent harassment, abuse, or severe misconduct.  
**Consequence:** Permanent removal from the community.
